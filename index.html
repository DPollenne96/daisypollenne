I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at INSEAD in the Organisational Behaviour area, jointly affiliated with the Stone Centre for the Study of Wealth Inequality and the INSEAD Gender Initiative. My research examines the relation between work and well-being, with particular focus on how workplace demands and stressors are unequally distributed across individuals. I also investigate how unique organisational structures and cultural norms shape individuals' experiences in their professional environments.

My academic foundation comes from the University of Oxford, where I earned my Ph.D. in Social Anthropology. Through field observations and in-depth interviews with refugees in the UK, I explored the meaning of well-being after seeking asylum. Further, I examined how refugees' well-being was affected by their limited access to and concentration in certain occupational fields. This work sparked my current focus on well-being in organisational contexts.

At INSEAD, I am developing several research streams. Working with Maria Guadalupe and Kaisa Snellman, I use novel survey data to explore how hyper-competitive corporate cultures affect both individual well-being and organisational performance. In a separate project with Kourtney Koebel, I examine how the transition to motherhood impacts women's employment trajectories and well-being, using large-scale administrative panel data.

My research aims to understand and address the complex intersections of work, well-being, and inequality. I welcome opportunities to collaborate on these issues!
