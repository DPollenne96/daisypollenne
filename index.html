About:

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Stone Centre for the Study of Wealth Inequality at INSEAD. My research examines the relationship between employment and well-being, focusing on how organisational structures and cultural norms shape individuals' experiences in professional environments.

I hold a Ph.D. in Social Anthropology from the University of Oxford, where my research on refugee well-being in the UK labour market examined tensions between immediate employment needs and refugees' long-term integration. This work has inspired a shift to my current focus, on well-being in organisational contexts.

At INSEAD, I am currently developing several research streams. The first explores the potential trade-offs for organisations in promoting individual and collective purpose, and how such tensions affect individuals' well-being and performance. The second examines well-being implications of changes in women's employment careers during the transition to motherhood.
